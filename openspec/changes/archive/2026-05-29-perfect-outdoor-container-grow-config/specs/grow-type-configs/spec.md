## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Outdoor Container Heat Management
The system SHALL provide container heat management as the #1 outdoor container concern, including root zone temperature thresholds (slows >85°F, death >95°F), solutions (white/light pots, pot sleeves, double-potting, partial burial, shade cloth, mulch), and monitoring guidance.

#### Scenario: Dark pots in direct summer sun
- **WHEN** an outdoor container grower has dark-colored pots in direct afternoon sun
- **THEN** the system warns about root zone overheating, recommends: white pot sleeves or wraps (reflective), double-pot with air gap, 3-4 inches mulch on soil surface, shade cloth on south-facing side, consider replacing with light-colored fabric pots, and monitor soil temperature (insert probe 3 inches deep)

### Requirement: Outdoor Container Root Binding and Pot Sizing
The system SHALL provide container sizing guide for outdoor plants: autos (5-10 gal), photos (15-50+ gal for full outdoor size), root binding signs, fabric pot air pruning benefits, and up-potting schedule throughout the grow.

#### Scenario: Outdoor photo plant showing slowed growth in July
- **WHEN** a 6-foot outdoor plant in a 10 gal pot shows slowed growth despite adequate feeding
- **THEN** the system diagnoses likely root binding: outdoor photos need 15-50 gal for full size, recommends up-potting to at least 20 gal (fabric pot preferred for air pruning), warns about transplant shock timing (better to up-pot earlier or commit to final pot size from start), and suggests root pruning if up-potting mid-flower is too risky

### Requirement: Container Mobility Strategy
The system SHALL guide growers on the mobility advantage of containers: when to move for frost protection, storm shelter, light dep, sun optimization, and heat relief. Includes logistics: weight planning (wet 30 gal = 150+ lbs), dollies, wheeled platforms.

#### Scenario: Frost warning with outdoor container plants
- **WHEN** overnight temperatures forecast below 35°F for outdoor container plants
- **THEN** the system recommends: move containers to garage or covered porch (containers freeze faster than in-ground), if too heavy to move then insulate with bubble wrap around pots and cover plants with row covers, resume outdoor placement when overnight lows consistently above 40°F

### Requirement: Outdoor Container Accelerated Drying Management
The system SHALL address the 2-3x faster drying rate of outdoor containers vs in-ground, including monitoring methods (daily weight, moisture sensors), solutions (mulch, drip irrigation, self-watering inserts, water retention amendments), and twice-daily watering guidance for peak summer.

#### Scenario: Outdoor containers drying out daily in summer
- **WHEN** outdoor containers are drying completely within 24 hours during peak summer
- **THEN** the system provides: add 3-4 inches mulch on soil surface, install drip irrigation on timer (morning + evening during heat waves), ensure fabric pots are grouped for mutual shade, add water-retaining amendments to soil (vermiculite, coco coir), and check that pot size is adequate (small pots dry faster — consider up-potting)

### Requirement: Wind Protection for Outdoor Containers
The system SHALL provide wind protection guidance for tall outdoor plants in containers: wide-base pot selection, staking anchored to ground, grouping pots for mutual protection, windbreak placement, and securing for severe weather.

#### Scenario: Tall plant in container tipped by wind
- **WHEN** an outdoor container plant is knocked over by wind
- **THEN** the system recommends: stake with support anchored to ground or permanent structure (not the pot itself), use wide-base pots (wider than tall), group pots together for mutual wind protection, place windbreak on prevailing wind side, and for large plants secure pot to ground with ratchet straps for storm forecasts

### Requirement: Pot Material and Color Guide
The system SHALL provide outdoor-specific pot material comparison: fabric (best air pruning, dries fastest, no heat buildup — outdoor standard), plastic (cheap, retains moisture, overheats), ceramic/terracotta (heavy/stable, breathes, cracks in frost), Air Pots (excellent pruning, expensive). Color guidance: light colors preferred outdoors.

#### Scenario: Grower choosing pots for outdoor container grow
- **WHEN** a grower is selecting containers for an outdoor grow
- **THEN** the system recommends fabric pots (Smart Pots, Root Pouch) as the outdoor standard: air pruning prevents root circling, excellent drainage, lightweight for mobility, won't overheat in sun, and suggests white or tan color for heat climates. Warns against dark plastic in direct sun (root zone overheating) and terracotta in frost-prone areas (cracking)

### Requirement: Outdoor Container Stage-by-Stage Configuration
The system SHALL provide 12-stage grow configuration for outdoor containers with weather-integrated container-specific guidance, heat management per stage, pot size progression (up-potting schedule), and accelerated drying rate adjustments.

#### Scenario: Outdoor container grower in mid-summer veg
- **WHEN** an outdoor container grow is in vegetative stage during peak summer
- **THEN** the system provides: daily watering minimum (possibly twice in extreme heat), heat management active (check root zone temp daily), up-pot if needed before flower begins, ensure final pot size is adequate for expected plant size, wind staking for plants >3 feet, companion pest control active, and natural photoperiod monitoring (approaching flower trigger date)
