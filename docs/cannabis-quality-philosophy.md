# Cannabis-First Quality Philosophy

Tendril is a **cannabis-first platform**. Every feature — AI recommendations, sensor alerts, dashboard indicators, automation rules, and grow type configurations — is designed specifically for cannabis cultivation with quality as the top priority.

## Priority Order

All AI responses, recommendations, alerts, and automated actions follow this strict priority:

| Priority | Focus | Description |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Plant Health & Resin Production** | Healthy, unstressed plants channel energy into trichome development |
| 2 | **Terpene Preservation & Expression** | Protect volatile terpenes with proper environment, timing, and handling |
| 3 | **Cannabinoid Maturity & Potency** | Proper harvest windows, trichome development stages, never rush |
| 4 | **Bag Appeal & Bud Structure** | Dense, frosty, properly developed and cured flower |
| 5 | **Yield** | Dead last — never recommend actions that trade quality for quantity |

!!! warning "Quality Over Yield — Always"
    Tendril will **never** recommend higher EC, more light stress, or environmental shortcuts that sacrifice terpene profiles, trichome integrity, or cannabinoid potency in exchange for more weight. If you want to maximize yield at quality's expense, this platform will push back.

## Research-Validated Ideal Ranges

These ranges are enforced across all AI outputs, dashboard indicators, automation alerts, and grow type configurations. They are based on:

- **Dr. Bruce Bugbee** (Utah State University Crop Physiology Lab) — cannabis cultivation research
- **Dissolved oxygen solubility curves** — water temperature vs. DO availability
- **Pythium/root pathogen growth studies** — temperature-dependent pathogen proliferation
- **Cannabis terpene volatilization data** — monoterpene boiling points and degradation rates
- **VPD (Vapor Pressure Deficit) science** — transpiration optimization for cannabis
- **Botrytis cinerea germination studies** — humidity thresholds for bud rot prevention

### Water Temperature (Hydroponic Systems)

| Zone | Range | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| Ideal | 66–68°F (19–20°C) | Maximum dissolved oxygen, minimal pathogen risk |
| Acceptable | 64–70°F (18–21°C) | Green zone — no action needed |
| Caution | 70–72°F (21–22°C) | Add Hydroguard, increase aeration, monitor roots |
| Warning | 72–75°F (22–24°C) | Active pathogen growth conditions — reduce immediately |
| Critical | >75°F (>24°C) | Root damage in progress regardless of supplements |

**Why it matters:** Dissolved oxygen drops ~2mg/L between 65°F and 75°F. Cannabis roots are especially susceptible to Pythium oomycetes which proliferate rapidly above 72°F. In DWC/RDWC systems, the reservoir is the plant's lifeline — keeping it cool is non-negotiable for quality.

#### Aeroponics Root Zone

Misted roots are more exposed and require slightly cooler conditions:

- **Target:** 65°F (18°C)
- **Acceptable:** 62–68°F (17–20°C)
- **Maximum:** 68°F (20°C)

### pH Ranges by Medium

| Medium | Range | Sweet Spot | Notes |
|--------|-------|------------|-------|
| Hydroponic (DWC, NFT, etc.) | 5.5–6.2 | 5.8 | Iron/manganese lockout above 6.3 |
| Coco Coir | 5.8–6.3 | 6.0 | Coco has natural cation exchange capacity |
| Soil | 6.0–7.0 | 6.5 | Microbial activity optimal in this range |
| Living Soil | 6.2–6.8 | 6.5 | Preserve soil food web |

**pH drift monitoring:** A drift >0.5 in 24 hours indicates a problem. Rising pH = plant consuming more nutrients (anions). Falling pH = plant consuming more water (cations).

### EC (Electrical Conductivity) by Stage

| Stage | EC Range | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Seedling | 0.2–0.5 | Barely any nutrients — roots are fragile |
| Early Veg | 0.8–1.2 | Ramp slowly as root mass develops |
| Late Veg | 1.2–1.4 | Full vegetative growth, N-heavy |
| Transition | 1.4–1.8 | Begin P/K ramp, reduce N 20–30% |
| Early Flower | 1.4–1.8 | P/K ramp, maintain CalMag under LED |
| Mid Flower | 1.6–2.0 | Peak nutrient demand — this is the MAXIMUM |
| Late Flower | 1.0–1.4 | Begin reduction for clean fade |
| Flush/Ripening | 0.0–0.4 | Plain water or very light feed |

!!! danger "EC Above 2.2 = Yield Chasing"
    EC values above 2.2 in cannabis cultivation prioritize biomass over quality. Salt stress from high EC:

    - Destroys volatile terpenes (especially monoterpenes like myrcene and limonene)
    - Causes nutrient lockout and tip burn
    - Produces harsh, chemical-tasting flower
    - Reduces trichome head size and density

    Tendril will flag EC above 2.0 and actively warn above 2.2.

### Air Temperature by Stage

| Stage | Day (Lights On) | Night (Lights Off) | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|--------------------| ------|
| Vegetative | 70–85°F (21–30°C) | 65–72°F (18–22°C) | Warmer temps drive faster growth |
| Early Flower | 72–78°F (22–26°C) | 62–68°F (17–20°C) | Begin cooling for quality |
| Mid Flower | 72–78°F (22–26°C) | 60–68°F (16–20°C) | DIF (day/night differential) enhances terpenes |
| Late Flower | 68–75°F (20–24°C) | 58–65°F (14–18°C) | Protect volatile terpenes from heat |
| Ripening | 65–72°F (18–22°C) | 55–62°F (13–17°C) | Maximum terpene retention, color expression |

**Day/Night Differential (DIF):** A 10–15°F drop at night:

- Triggers anthocyanin production (purple/blue coloring in compatible genetics)
- Enhances terpene retention (volatile terps off-gas faster in heat)
- Improves bud density
- Mimics natural autumn conditions that signal ripening

!!! tip "Temperature After Week 6 of Flower"
    After week 6, terpene content ramps significantly. Heat above 80°F during this period literally evaporates your terpenes into the air. This is where many growers unknowingly lose 20–40% of their potential aroma and flavor profile.

### Humidity by Stage

| Stage | Range | VPD Target | Risk Above Max |
|-------|-------|------------|----------------|
| Clones/Seedlings | 65–80% | 0.4–0.8 kPa | Mold on dome |
| Vegetative | 60–70% | 0.8–1.2 kPa | Slow growth |
| Transition | 45–55% | 1.0–1.4 kPa | Early botrytis setup |
| Early Flower | 45–55% | 1.0–1.4 kPa | Powdery mildew |
| Mid Flower | 40–50% | 1.0–1.4 kPa | Botrytis on dense buds |
| Late Flower | 35–45% | 0.8–1.2 kPa | Bud rot — devastating |
| Ripening/Flush | 30–40% | 0.8–1.0 kPa | Mold in dense colas |

!!! danger "Humidity Above 55% in Flower = Botrytis Risk"
    Dense cannabis buds trap moisture between calyxes. Botrytis cinerea (grey mold/bud rot) spores germinate readily above 55% RH on flower tissue. A single infected bud can spread to an entire canopy overnight. This is the #1 harvest-destroying pathogen in cannabis cultivation.

### Dissolved Oxygen

| Level | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| >8 mg/L | Excellent | Optimal root health and nutrient uptake |
| 6–8 mg/L | Good | Acceptable — ensure adequate aeration |
| 5–6 mg/L | Low | Add air stones, check water temp |
| <5 mg/L | Critical | Root health emergency — immediate action |

The health check AI scores your grow based on how well the environment supports **quality flower production**, not biomass. A grow with perfect EC at 1.8 and 68°F water will score higher than one pushing EC 2.6 at 74°F — even if the latter produces more weight.

Scoring criteria:

- Sensor readings vs. cannabis-optimal ranges (not generic plant ranges)
- Environmental conditions for terpene preservation
- Visual symptoms (trichome development, leaf health, pest/pathogen indicators)
- Nutrient status for quality (not maximum growth rate)
- Root health indicators

- Flag water temp >70°F immediately
- Recommend EC reductions if above 2.0 in flower
- Suggest temperature drops in late flower for terpene preservation
- Never recommend actions that sacrifice quality for speed or weight
- Reference your specific equipment when giving advice

- Suggests proper flush timing for clean-burning flower
- Accounts for LED-specific CalMag needs

- Prioritizes terpene-protective feeding over maximum growth rate

## The Science Behind the Numbers

### Why 68°F Water, Not 72°F?

At 68°F:
- Dissolved oxygen: ~8.5 mg/L (at sea level)
- Pythium growth rate: minimal
- Root metabolic activity: optimal
- Nutrient uptake efficiency: peak

At 72°F:
- Dissolved oxygen: ~7.8 mg/L (−8%)
- Pythium growth rate: accelerating
- Root stress begins
- Beneficial bacteria (Bacillus) less competitive

At 75°F:
- Dissolved oxygen: ~7.2 mg/L (−15%)
- Pythium growth rate: rapid
- Root rot likely without heavy intervention
- Nutrient uptake compromised

### Why EC 2.0 Max, Not 2.6?

Cannabis trichome heads are fragile structures filled with cannabinoids and terpenes. High EC creates osmotic stress that:

1. Reduces water flow to trichome stalks (less resin production)
2. Causes salt accumulation in leaf tissue (nutrient burn)
3. Triggers stress responses that divert energy from secondary metabolite production
4. Produces flower that tests high in certain cannabinoids but smokes harsh due to mineral content

Award-winning craft cannabis is consistently grown at moderate EC (1.4–1.8 peak) with proper flush timing — not at maximum nutrient concentration.

### Why Temperature Drops in Late Flower?

Cannabis evolved in the Hindu Kush mountains and Central Asian highlands where autumn brings significant temperature drops. The plant interprets cooler nights as a signal to:

1. **Increase resin production** — trichomes serve as UV protection and insulation
2. **Concentrate terpenes** — cooler temperatures reduce volatilization of monoterpenes
3. **Express anthocyanins** — purple/blue pigments triggered by cool nights (genetics permitting)
4. **Slow metabolic rate** — allows more complete biosynthesis of complex terpenes and cannabinoids
